## Note Reference

### Indian to Western Note Mapping (Sa = C)

| Swara | Symbol | Western Note | Interval |
|-------|--------|--------------|----------|
| Shadja (Sa) | S | C | Tonic |
| Komal Rishabh | r | Db | Minor 2nd |
| Shuddha Rishabh | R | D | Major 2nd |
| Komal Gandhar | g | Eb | Minor 3rd |
| Shuddha Gandhar | G | E | Major 3rd |
| Shuddha Madhyam | m | F | Perfect 4th |
| Tivra Madhyam | M | F# | Augmented 4th |
| Pancham (Pa) | P | G | Perfect 5th |
| Komal Dhaivat | d | Ab | Minor 6th |
| Shuddha Dhaivat | D | A | Major 6th |
| Komal Nishad | n | Bb | Minor 7th |
| Shuddha Nishad | N | B | Major 7th |

### Notation Key
- **Uppercase** = Shuddha (natural)
- **Lowercase** = Komal (flat)
- **M** = Tivra Madhyam (sharp 4th)
- **~** = Andolan (oscillation)
- **/** = Meend (glide up)
- **\\** = Meend (glide down)
- **( )** = Kan swara (grace note)
- **Varjit** = Omitted note

## Notes on Performance

1. **Samay (Time)**: Traditional performers strictly follow time theory
2. **Andolan**: Many ragas require oscillation of specific notes (especially komal Re, komal Ga, komal Dha)
3. **Meend**: Gliding between notes is essential for raga character
4. **Pakad**: Each raga has a signature phrase that identifies it
5. **Vadi-Samvadi**: Most important and second-most important notes define the raga's center
